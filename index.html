The 2060 Settlement Atlas

A SPECULATIVE WORLDBUILDING PROJECT

Imagine feeding and housing ~4 billion people in 2060 under strict rules: no fossil fuels, no synthetic fertilizer or pesticide, minimal imports. Where could people sustainably live?

This map is one answer. Each colored region is a proposed settlement zone; the vast unmarked space is wilderness by design — left wild so the settled land can be farmed sustainably.

Use the buttons at the bottom-left to toggle layers:

  • Exclusions — where a worst-case 2060 climate makes settlement unviable (heat, drowned coasts, drought)
  • Openings — land that becomes newly habitable as the world warms
  • Cities — 474 metros: green = viable, orange = heat-stressed, gold = defended (extreme-heat refuge), red = excluded. Hover any city for its 2060 wet-bulb verdict.

Heat verdicts use the empirical human wet-bulb limit (~31°C, PSU HEAT Project) — the point past which healthy adults can't safely work outdoors. Click a region for its full profile. It's a thought experiment, not a prediction.
